## Were your predictions right? 
NO, all three of my predictins turned out to be wrong. I guesses Friday would have the most screen time, but Wednesday actually had the most with 8 hours.
I also predicted Saturday would be my best focus day since I'd be more rested, but Thursday actually had the highest focus level of 9. Even with slightly 
less sleep than the weekend. And I assumed more caffiene would mean better focus especially on days with less sleep, but the data showed the opposite. My 
high-caffeine days actually averaged lower focus than my low-caffiene days.


## What suprised you? 
The caffeine results suprised me the most. I've always fallen back on caffeine to help me on those days where I get less sleep, but comparing the two groups of days
showed my focus was actually higher on the days I drank less caffeine. It makes me wonder if the days I intake more caffiene are already those days that were less sleep, 
more stressful, and low focus to being with. So the caffiene isn't causing the low focus, but more of a corrolation. 

## What was tricky or rewarding? 
The trickiest part of the code was writing the correlateCaffeieneToFocus(). At first I wasn't sure hjow to compare something that doesn't have an obvious cutoff, so I had to think through splitting the days into groups
(high caffeine vs. low caffeine) and averaging each group separately instead of just comparing two single numbers. It was satisfying once it worked, since it was the first time my code revealed a pattern I couldn't see
just by looking at raw data.
